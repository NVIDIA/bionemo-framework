Gradient Underflows Are Not the Problem

The existing gradient underflow heatmaps show values <0.05% across all layers — well within acceptable range. I added activation underflow analysis to look deeper, and found the real issue.

Severe underflow at the last FP8 layer.

The fc2 component (SwiGLU down-projection) at the last FP8 layer before the BF16 boundary appears to have extreme dynamic range that block scaling struggles to represent.

fc2 Activation Underflow Heatmaps

FL2: Layers 3–30 in FP8. Layer 30 reaches 18.6% underflow.

FL4: Layers 5–28 in FP8. Layer 28 reaches 16.1% underflow.

The degradation is concentrated at the last FP8 layer in both configs. All other layers are healthy (<0.03%). The boundary effect appears to follow the last FP8 layer regardless of where it's placed.

fc2 Activation MSE (Quantization Error)

FL2: Layer 30 MSE reaches 37M. All others near zero.

FL4: Layer 28 MSE reaches 133K. All others near zero.

The quantization error at the boundary is orders of magnitude higher than in all other layers. Despite FL2 having worse MSE (37M vs 133K), it converges better — suggesting the convergence gap is likely not driven by boundary precision alone.

Boundary Layer Degradation Over Time

FL2: Layer 30 underflow grows from ~8% to ~18% over 22K steps.

FL4: Layer 28 underflow grows from ~4% to ~16% over 12K steps.

Top: fc2 activation underflow %. Bottom: fc2 activation MSE. Both grow steadily over training for the last FP8 layer, while adjacent layers remain flat. This suggests a progressive degradation at the precision boundary.

Summary & Next Steps

What the results suggest

  • Block scaling has difficulty with fc2 activation dynamic range at the FP8→BF16 boundary
  • The boundary effect is localized to ~1 layer and appears position-independent
  • Adding more BF16 layers (FL4) doesn't address the boundary issue and may hurt by reducing efficient FP8 compute
  • Gradient underflows are not a concern for this model at this scale

Caveats

  • FL2/FL4 used the original metagenome dataset (not the corrected reshuffled parquet2)
  • FL4 ran for fewer steps (~36K vs ~49K)
